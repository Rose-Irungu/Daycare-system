By the Age of 2

At two, your child is becoming more aware of their surroundings and shows increased interaction. Here’s what most two-year-olds typically accomplish after our program.

1

Mastering Basic Motor Skills

Toddlers develop essential motor skills through activities like crawling, walking, and climbing. These skills are fundamental for independence and building coordination, allowing them to engage with their environment and learn more effectively.

2

Cognitive Development

At this stage, children begin to understand the world around them. Through play, exploration, and problem-solving activities, toddlers enhance their memory, critical thinking, and ability to focus. Engaging in activities that stimulate the mind helps boost cognitive skills that will support future learning.

3

Language Progression

Toddlers are in the early stages of language development. Listening to stories, interacting with caregivers, and mimicking sounds help to expand their vocabulary. Social interactions also promote language skills as they begin to express needs and thoughts.

4

Exploring Self-Expression

Through art, music, and movement, toddlers explore different ways of expressing themselves. These activities encourage creativity and allow children to communicate their emotions and ideas, enhancing their sense of identity and individuality.

5

Social Engagement

Learning to interact with others is a key milestone during toddlerhood. Engaging in group activities, taking turns, and learning empathy helps toddlers build social skills. This foundation prepares them for future relationships and teamwork.

6

Building Self-Sufficiency

Encouraging toddlers to do tasks independently, such as feeding themselves, dressing, or tidying up, fosters self-confidence and responsibility. These activities teach them the importance of self-care and the skills necessary to manage daily tasks.
